The single highest-signal check on a California DL/ID is barcode-to-face consistency. A skilled forger will alter the printed fields but routinely neglect the PDF417 barcode on the back, or vice versa. Always treat any mismatch between the printed name, DOB, DL number, sex, eye color, height, or expiration date and their corresponding AAMVA barcode fields (DCS/DAC/DAD, DBB, DAQ, DBC, DAY, DAU, DBA, DBD) as near-certain tampering. The barcode header must begin with the '@' character followed immediately by '636014' — California's AAMVA issuer identification number. Any other IIN, or a QR code substituted for a PDF417, is disqualifying.
The DL number format is one uppercase Latin letter followed by exactly seven digits — eight characters total, no spaces or hyphens, no second alpha character. Forgers frequently produce nine-character numbers or use numeric-only strings copied from other states. The label on the card face must read 'DRIVER LICENSE' — no apostrophe, no 'S' — and the card type text must appear in blue for a driver license or green for an identification card. Discrepancies here are common on high-quality fakes that import layouts from other states.
Generation confusion is a persistent failure mode. Gen 3 cards (issued through September 2025) carry a magnetic strip on the back and a single PDF417 barcode. Gen 4 cards (October 2025 onward) remove the magnetic strip entirely and add a second barcode carrying a digital signature; a Gen 4 background shows a redwood forest and Pacific coastline, while Gen 3 shows a Gold Rush miner at right with poppies lower left. A card displaying Gen 4 imagery but still bearing a magnetic strip, or a Gen 3 background with two barcodes, is internally inconsistent.
The tactile DOB printed in raised three-dimensional numerals across the primary photo is extremely difficult to replicate in a printed or digitally-altered forgery — in images, look for the numerals sitting visually above the photo layer rather than flat against it. The REAL ID bear-and-star mark (gold bear silhouette with white star interior) must appear on all post-January 2018 compliant cards; if absent, 'FEDERAL LIMITS APPLY' must substitute — both absent simultaneously on a modern card is a red flag. For under-21 cards, the age bar year must arithmetically equal the cardholder's 21st birth year derived from their printed DOB; a one-year-off error is the most common semantic mistake forgers make when constructing synthetic identities.
