The highest-signal single check on any California DL/ID is barcode-to-face cross-consistency.
A competent forger alters the printed surface but rarely synchronizes every AAMVA PDF417 field — prioritize comparing DAQ (license number) against the printed DL number, DBB (DOB) against the printed DOB, and DCS/DAC/DAD (name fields) against the printed name.
Any one mismatch is near-certain tampering.
Equally, the barcode header must open with '@' followed immediately by '636014' — California's AAMVA issuer identification number — before any other data; a different IIN or a QR code in place of a stacked PDF417 is disqualifying regardless of how clean the card face looks.
The DL number is exactly eight characters: one uppercase Latin letter followed by seven digits, no spaces, hyphens, or second alpha character.
Forgers copying formats from other states frequently produce nine characters or all-numeric strings.
The card type label must read 'DRIVER LICENSE' — no apostrophe, no trailing 'S' — rendered in blue for a driver license and green for an identification card.
These color and text details are persistently wrong on fakes assembled from out-of-state templates.
Generation consistency is a structural trap.
Gen 3 cards (through September 2025) show a Gold Rush miner at right with poppies lower left on the background, carry a magnetic strip on the back, and have a single PDF417.
Gen 4 cards (October 2025 onward) replace that imagery with a redwood forest and Pacific coastline, remove the magnetic strip entirely, and add a second barcode on the back bearing a digital signature.
A card combining Gen 4 scenery with a magnetic strip, or Gen 3 scenery with two barcodes, is internally incoherent — this is one of the most common failures when forgers apply a new-generation overlay to an older card body.
For under-21 cards, the red age bar must read 'AGE 21 IN [YEAR]' where the year is arithmetically derived from the printed DOB, not copied from another card. A one-year offset — the most common synthetic-identity error — is detectable by adding 21 to the birth year and comparing. REAL ID compliance requires a gold bear silhouette with a white star inside; if that mark is absent, the text 'FEDERAL LIMITS APPLY' must appear near the top.
Both absent simultaneously on any post-January 2018 card is a hard red flag.
Visually, the tactile DOB numerals printed across the primary photo should appear to sit above the photo plane — in images look for the slight shadow or embossed relief that distinguishes laser engraving from a flat printed overlay. The ghost photo is grayscale and smaller, typically positioned in the upper-right area of the card face; a ghost photo that matches the primary photo's tonal range rather than appearing distinctly desaturated is a forgery indicator. Document Discriminator field DCF in the barcode must match the DD printed on the card back — forgers who edit one almost never update both.
